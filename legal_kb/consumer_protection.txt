# Consumer Protection

## Overview
Consumer protection law safeguards consumers against unfair trade practices, defective goods, misleading advertisements, and deficient services.

## Applicable Acts
- Consumer Protection Act, 2019
- Legal Metrology Act, 2009

## Risk Indicators
- Hidden charges
- Misleading advertisements
- Unfair contract terms
- No refund policy

## Practical Review Checklist
- Identify the parties.
- Verify dates and duration.
- Review financial obligations.
- Examine termination conditions.
- Check governing law and dispute resolution.
- Review confidentiality and liability.
- Confirm signatures and execution requirements.


## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: What should be reviewed first?
A: Parties, obligations, payment terms, duration, and termination.

Q: What makes a clause risky?
A: Ambiguous language, unlimited liability, unilateral rights, or missing remedies.

Q: Should AI replace legal advice?
A: No. AI can summarize and highlight issues but should not replace professional legal advice.
