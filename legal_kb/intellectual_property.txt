# Intellectual Property

## Overview
Intellectual Property (IP) protects creations of the mind, including inventions, literary works, artistic works, trademarks, industrial designs, and trade secrets. It enables creators and businesses to control and license the use of their work.

## Applicable Acts
- Copyright Act, 1957
- Patents Act, 1970
- Trade Marks Act, 1999
- Designs Act, 2000

## Practical Guidance
- Identify parties and responsibilities.
- Review timelines and deadlines.
- Verify financial obligations.
- Check governing law and dispute resolution.
- Ensure signatures and execution requirements are complete.

## Risk Indicators
- Unclear ownership
- Missing license scope
- Trademark misuse
- Unauthorized copying
